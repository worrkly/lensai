How it works

Three steps to the truth

LensAI uses Claude AI to analyse the statistical and visual patterns that separate human-created content from machine-generated output.

1
Choose your content type
Select a detection mode — Text, Image, Audio, Code, or Video URL. Each mode uses a specialised set of detection signals tuned for that content type.
2
Paste, upload or describe
Paste text directly, upload an image file, describe an audio clip, drop in code, or share a video link. No account or sign-up required — just submit and go.
3
Get your instant verdict
Within 3 seconds you receive an AI probability score (0–100%), a verdict label, a per-signal breakdown with explanations, and a written analysis you can export or copy.

About LensAI

Built to preserve what's human

LensAI is a free AI content detection tool built by worrkly — a platform dedicated to smarter, more human ways of working. As AI-generated content becomes harder to distinguish from human-created work, we built Lens to give everyone a reliable, accessible way to check.

The tool analyses content across five dimensions — text, images, audio, code, and video — using Claude AI as its detection engine. Each mode applies a specialised set of signals: from perplexity and burstiness in writing, to diffusion model artefacts in images, to boilerplate patterns in code.

LensAI is intentionally free and open, with no account required. We believe AI detection should be a public good — accessible to educators, journalists, recruiters, developers, and anyone who wants to understand the content they're reading or receiving.

Results are probabilistic, not absolute. We always recommend applying human judgement alongside any detection score. Our tool is a starting point for inquiry, not a final verdict.

FAQ

Common questions

How accurate is LensAI?
Accuracy varies by content type and length. Text detection performs best on samples over 200 words. Image detection relies on visual artefact analysis by Claude. All results are probabilistic — a score of 85% means the content shows strong AI signals, not that it is definitely AI-generated. Always apply human judgement alongside any result.
Why is there a weekly scan limit?
Each scan calls the Claude AI API, which has a real cost. The 2 scans per week limit keeps the tool free and sustainable for everyone. Your limit resets automatically every Monday at midnight UTC. No account is needed — limits are tracked by IP address.
Is my content stored or used for training?
Your content is sent to Anthropic's Claude API for analysis and is subject to Anthropic's privacy policy. LensAI itself does not store the content of your scans. Scan metadata (type, timestamp, IP) may be logged for rate limiting and admin purposes only.
What content types does it support?
LensAI supports five detection modes: Text (paste any written content), Image (upload JPG, PNG, WEBP, GIF), Audio (paste a transcript or describe what you heard), Code (paste any programming language), and Video URL (enter a link to a public video for URL-pattern analysis).
Can it detect mixed human and AI content?
Yes. Scores in the 35–68% range are classified as "Uncertain" — this often indicates content that blends human and AI writing, or that has been lightly edited after AI generation. The signal breakdown helps you understand which specific dimensions triggered the score.
Does it work for languages other than English?
Since the detection engine is Claude AI, it supports a wide range of languages. However, signal accuracy may vary for non-English content as the underlying models are trained predominantly on English data. Results for non-English text should be treated with additional caution.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2025

LensAI ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights in relation to it.

1. Information we collect

LensAI is designed to be as privacy-preserving as possible. We do not require you to create an account or provide personal information to use the tool.

  • Content you submit: Text, images, audio descriptions, code, or video URLs you submit are sent directly to Anthropic's Claude API for analysis. We do not store the content of your submissions on our servers.
  • Usage data: We collect your IP address for the purpose of enforcing the weekly scan limit. IP addresses are stored in server memory and reset periodically. They are masked in our admin systems.

Your submitted content is processed by Anthropic's Claude API. By using LensAI, your content is subject to Anthropic's privacy policy and terms of service. We encourage you to review Anthropic's policies at anthropic.com.

LensAI is hosted on Vercel. Vercel may collect standard server logs including IP addresses and request metadata as part of their infrastructure operations.

4. Data retention

We do not retain the content of your scans. IP-based rate limit data is stored in server memory and is lost when the server restarts. We do not maintain a permanent database of user submissions.

5. Your rights

Since we do not store personal data linked to identifiable users, there is limited data to request, correct, or delete. If you have concerns about data processed by Anthropic, please contact Anthropic directly.
